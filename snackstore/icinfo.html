ABOUT

Little Pudding

The name comes from its milky, pudding-like flavor.

A creamy ice pop that tastes like sweet milk dessert.

Ice Factory

Sounds industrial, but it’s just a fruity ice pop.

have a lot of different flavors. The picture is pineapple flavor, icy and refreshing instead of creamy.

Old Popsicle

A classic, old-style ice pop made from frozen sugar water.

Long before creamy ice cream was common, this was the cheap, everyday summer treat, simple, sweet, and refreshing.

Green Mood

Named for its mung-bean flavor.

Not creamy, more like frozen sweet mung-bean soup (or flavored syrup) made into an icy popsicle.

Milky Grape

Milk plus grape flavored chunks in ice cream form.

Sweet, creamy, with little grape pieces that taste like candy.

Silly Nana

A banana-shaped pop that you can “peel.”

The outer shell is jelly that peels like banana skin, and inside is soft banana-flavored ice cream.

Three-Color Cup

Exactly what it sounds like, three flavors in one cup.

Often chocolate, strawberry, and vanilla, scooped together in stripes.

Snow Lotus

Despite the name, it has nothing to do with real lotus.

They’re small icy chunks made from lemon-flavored sugar water, refreshing, with no milk at all.

Little Snowy

Little Snowy “小雪生 (xiaoxuesheng)” and “小学生” are pronounced the same in Chinese, so it's shaped like a little kid.

Two layers: chocolate on top, plain milk ice cream below.

Corn Delight

It looks like a corn cob, but it’s just a regular cone on the outside.

Inside is plain milk ice cream, no corn flavor at all.

Green Tongue

A green apple–flavored popsicle that slowly softens as you eat it,

turning jelly-like, just like a “tongue.”
