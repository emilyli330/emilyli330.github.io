Explosive Fruit Soda

The name promises an explosion，and it kind of delivers.

They’re fizzy, popping candy pieces that “explode” on your tongue like tiny fireworks.

Rainbow Candy Ropes

Named for its bright colors that look like rainbows.

It’s a stretchy, chewy sour candy rope you can twist, pull, or just eat in one bite.

Little Stone Beans

They look like glossy chocolate pebbles, hence the “stone” name.

Inside is chocolate; outside is a crunchy shell that makes them look like real rocks

Taro Candy

Simple name, simple snack.

These are small chewy squares flavored with taro， soft, mild, and very old-school.

Sour Girl

Based on a saying in China: “sour for girls, spicy for boys.”

The name is just a joke, these are sour-sugar-coated gummies that make you pucker before they turn sweet.

Diamond Candy

Also called “Ring Candy,” because you can wear it like a ring.

A hard lollipop jewel you can put on your finger and lick like a “diamond.”

CC Fun

A nostalgic, powdery tablet candy.

It melts quickly on the tongue, sweet and chalky in the most old-school way.

Xuelizi

A marshmallow with a fancy name that means nothing about school.

Soft on the outside, filled with syrupy candy inside, luffy with a sweet liquid core.

Jelly Bubbles

Chewy jelly candy with watermelon flavor.

Bouncy, fruity, and shaped like little bubbly chunks.

Whistle Candy

Candy that doubles as a toy.

If you blow through the hole while it’s in your mouth, it whistles loudly — then you eat it.

Silly Bean

The name is goofy, like the candy itself.

Crunchy pea-shaped hard candies with super sour filling in the center.
