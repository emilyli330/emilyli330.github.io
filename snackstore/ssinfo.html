Zhenxiang Beef

Despite the name, it’s not real beef, just seasoned soy-based strips.

“真香” means “aromatic”, so the name refers to its flavor, not meat.

Green Crisp

A cilantro-flavored snack that looks neon green but tastes savory and spicy.

Its color comes from coriander seasoning, not artificial color.

Drunken Beast

Named after a “drunken hero”, but there’s no alcohol inside.

It’s just a fun name for a spicy soy snack focused purely on chili heat.

Indian Flying Cake

A thin, wide soy snack inspired by “roti prata”.

Light and crunchy, more like a giant chip than real cake.

The Return of the Condor Heroes

Named after a famous wuxia novel, but it’s just a soy-based spicy strip snack.

The two flavors (Hunan and Sichuan) simply highlight its spicy roots.

Vegetarian Spicy Chicken Tendon

Called “Chicken Tendon,” but it’s completely vegetarian.

Made from wheat gluten, shaped to imitate chewy meat texture.

Appetite Silk

The name simply means it’s meant to boost your appetite.

It’s a thin, chewy sour-and-spicy soy snack, with a sharper acidic kick than regular spicy strips.

Tang Monk’s Meat

Named after the monk Tang Seng from Journey to the West (西游记).

In mythology, eating the monk grants immortality, so the name is a joke.

The snack is vegan, made with seasoned textured soy protein.

Overlord Strips

The name sounds dramatic, but it has nothing to do with the snack.

Rice Noodles

The name comes from a famous noodle dish in China, but it has nothing to do with this snack.

This is simply a thin, slightly crispy soy-based spicy strip.
